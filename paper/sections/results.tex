\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Subject atlas: frozen FMs primarily encode subject
  identity}
\label{sec:results_atlas}

The subject-dominance hypothesis makes a structural prediction
testable independently of any classification outcome: if frozen EEG
FM representations are subject atlases, variance decomposition of
those representations should find that the subject factor accounts
for the majority of explainable variance and the task-label factor
for a small, near-chance slice. We test this across three FMs
(LaBraM, CBraMod, REVE) and four clinical EEG datasets
(UCSD Stress, EEGMAT, ADFTD, TDBRAIN) --- twelve model$\times$dataset
cells. For each cell we extract a single frozen embedding per
recording by global-mean-pooling the final-layer patch tokens of the
pretrained FM with no gradient flow, no classifier head, and no
fine-tuning.

\textbf{Nested variance decomposition.}
Table~\ref{tab:subject_atlas} reports the pooled label and subject
fractions per cell. The label fraction is below $6$\% in every cell;
the subject/label ratio is at least $10\times$ and in some cells
exceeds $50\times$. This is not a fringe property of one
(model, dataset) pair --- it is uniformly true for every combination
of FM and dataset tested, and therefore describes a property of
pretrained-FM representations of clinical EEG.

\textbf{RSA unanimity.} Across all 12 cells, the Spearman correlation
between the embedding RDM and the subject-identity RDM exceeds the
correlation between the embedding RDM and the label-identity RDM. No
cell falls on the label-dominant side of the diagonal. Five auxiliary
subject-separability metrics (silhouette, Fisher, nearest-neighbour
subject-recovery accuracy, LogME, H-score) agree in every cell; see
supplementary for the full tabulation.

\textbf{PermANOVA, where power permits.} On the three datasets where
$n$ is large enough for PermANOVA to have adequate power
(ADFTD, TDBRAIN), the subject-factor $p$-value from 499-permutation
PermANOVA is $\leq\!0.04$ for 5 of 6 model$\times$dataset cells. On
UCSD Stress ($n_\text{rec}=55$, $n_\text{subj}=14$), PermANOVA
$p$-values are uniformly $0.46$--$0.59$, consistent with known
small-sample power limits \citep{smalln2025biorxiv} rather than
absence of subject clustering --- variance decomposition and RSA
measures confirm the subject-dominant structure on UCSD Stress, they
merely cannot be bootstrapped to permutation-significance at $n = 55$.

\textbf{A partial exception consistent with --- though not a
  controlled test of --- the thesis.} One cell deserves separate
treatment. Under fine-tuning, \emph{LaBraM$\times$ADFTD} shifts its
pooled label fraction from $2.60\% \to 13.25\%$
($\Delta = +10.65$\,pp) and subject fraction from $66.8\% \to 60.0\%$.
This is the largest label-fraction increase in any cell we measured
and corresponds to a fine-tuned BA of $0.70$ (single-run; we did not
replicate this cell across multiple seeds, so the shift is reported
without variance). In every other cell, $\Delta$label-fraction is
$\leq\!+5.3$\,pp, and in four cells it is negative (the fine-tuning
objective pushed the representation further from the label axis while
still improving classifier-head BA). We read LaBraM$\times$ADFTD as
\emph{consistent with} SDL: the ADFTD label (Alzheimer's vs healthy
control) has a well-established cross-subject alpha-slowing signature
in the clinical EEG literature, so fine-tuning finds a label axis to
reorganise around. We note explicitly, however, that ADFTD is a
cross-subject categorical label rather than a within-subject paired
contrast, and we did not subject it to the same external anchoring
protocol applied to EEGMAT in Sec.~\ref{sec:results_paired}. It
therefore functions as a suggestive boundary observation, not a
second controlled test.
Sec.~\ref{sec:results_paired} operationalises the thesis via a paired
within-subject experiment where contrast strength is externally
anchored rather than inferred from FT outcomes.

\textbf{Takeaway.} Subject-dominance is a representation-level
structural property of pretrained EEG FM representations on clinical
EEG cohorts. Downstream classification on these representations is
constrained to work against a representation in which task-label
variance is a small minority of the signal.

\subsection{Subject-level evaluation audit of UCSD Stress}
\label{sec:results_audit}

Wang et al.\ \citep{wang2025stress}, working on the same
Komarov--Ko--Jung dataset, report $0.9047$ BA for fine-tuned LaBraM
under five-fold cross-validation. Inspection of their code shows
recording-level rather than subject-level fold assignment. On our
17-subject / 70-recording cohort where 15 of 17 subjects contribute
multiple recordings, this protocol produces subject leakage in every
fold. We re-run the same FMs on the same subset under two protocols
holding everything else fixed: trial-level StratifiedKFold(5)
(reproducing Wang's design) and subject-level StratifiedGroupKFold(5)
with \texttt{groups=patient\_id}. The comparison is a three-seed
mean $\pm$ sample standard deviation per model.
Fig.~\ref{fig:honest_funnel} summarises the cascade of corrections.

Every FM drops under subject-level CV, by a model-dependent amount. Our

\textbf{Classical baselines under class-balanced evaluation.} A

$5$\,pp gap lies entirely inside the $0.44$--$0.58$

sit a few pp higher within it.

\textbf{Permutation-null comparison.} For the LaBraM canonical
fine-tuning recipe on UCSD Stress: real FT $0.443 \pm 0.083$
(3 seeds) versus permutation-null FT $0.497 \pm 0.086$
(10 permutations) yields one-sided $p = 0.70$. Real labels produce a
\emph{numerically lower} BA than shuffled labels on this recipe. For
CBraMod and REVE, ten-permutation null tests bottom out at the
$p = 0.1$ floor, insufficient to distinguish small real effects from
seed noise; we flag these as underpowered.

This audit establishes that the ceiling exists; the controlled
paired comparison below distinguishes contrast strength from
alternative explanations.

\subsection{Paired within-subject contrast experiment: EEGMAT rescues,
  UCSD Stress does not}
\label{sec:results_paired}

The paired comparison has two arms using the same three FMs and the
same subject-respecting evaluation. Both are reported as three-seed
mean $\pm$ std. The \emph{only} structural difference between arms is
the neural contrast underlying the label. Following the contrast
anchoring protocol (Sec.~\ref{sec:methods_anchor}), contrast strength
is defined externally, before reading any FM result.

\textbf{EEGMAT anchor.} Serial-subtraction mental arithmetic produces
canonical posterior alpha desynchronisation
\citep{klimesch1999,pfurtscheller1979,klimesch2012update}. Robust,
spectrally localised, reliably induced across participants.

\textbf{UCSD Stress longitudinal DSS anchor.} A systematic search
returns no published per-recording within-subject EEG correlate of
DASS-21 or Daily Stress Scale scores. The Komarov--Ko--Jung paper
itself is a group-level longitudinal correlational study
(Sec.~\ref{sec:methods_dass_scope}), not a per-recording within-subject
classification benchmark.

As a pre-registered internal check (Sec.~\ref{sec:methods_anchor}), we
additionally tested whether the 70-recording cohort itself exhibits a
reproducible group-level spectral contrast between DASS-high
($n = 14$) and DASS-low ($n = 56$) recordings that could serve as an
independent external anchor. A cluster-based permutation $F$-test on
log-PSDs (MNE \texttt{permutation\_cluster\_test}, 1000 permutations,
default threshold at $p = 0.05$, 30 channels $\times$ 90 frequency
bins $0.5$--$45$\,Hz) returned \emph{no cluster-corrected significant
cluster} (minimum cluster $p = 0.096$). The largest subthreshold
cluster spans theta--alpha ($4$--$12$\,Hz) over 17 fronto-central
electrodes with within-cluster Hedges' $g = +0.82$, directionally
consistent with arousal-related spectral literature but not surviving
correction. Whole-scalp band-averaged effects were modest
(theta $g = +0.57$, alpha $g = +0.59$; delta/beta/gamma
$|g| \leq 0.13$). At the pre-registered threshold the external anchor
is therefore empirically absent on this cohort. Full details are
reported in the supplementary.

The contrast anchor in this arm is thus the \emph{absence} of both
(a) a published within-subject neural correlate in the literature and
(b) a cluster-corrected dataset-level spectral contrast in our own
pre-registered test. We note the subthreshold cluster as a scope
caveat: at $n = 14$ vs $n = 56$ the test may be underpowered, and the
directionally-consistent theta-alpha effect cannot be taken as
evidence of strong absence --- only as evidence that the contrast is
not robust enough to meet the pre-registered anchor criterion.

Under SDL the two anchors jointly predict the experimental outcome:
EEGMAT should rescue, Stress longitudinal DSS should fail.

\textbf{Arm 1 --- EEGMAT rescue succeeds.} Paired rest vs
serial-subtraction mental-arithmetic recordings for each of $36$
EEGMAT subjects ($72$ recordings). All three FMs fine-tuned on this
within-subject contrast rescue above classical and frozen-LP
baselines (3-seed mean $\pm$ std): LaBraM
$\mathrm{BA} = 0.731 \pm 0.021$, REVE $0.727 \pm 0.035$, CBraMod
$0.620 \pm 0.058$. The three-FM agreement is itself diagnostic ---
rescue is not a LaBraM-specific artefact but a consistent response
of pretrained EEG representations to an anchored within-subject
contrast. Critically, this rescue is \emph{not} accomplished by
rewriting the frozen representation to foreground
the label axis: as
reported in Table~\ref{tab:subject_atlas}, the pooled label fraction
shifts only from $5.35\% \to 3.34\%$ under FT on EEGMAT, and the
subject fraction increases from $71.2\% \to 82.8\%$. The rescue
operates at the classifier head while the embedding itself remains
subject-dominated, with the label axis projected onto the existing
subject-structured representation rather than reorganised around.
This is the ``projection, not rewrite'' mechanism.

\textbf{Arm 2 --- Stress longitudinal DSS fails.} We reframe UCSD
Stress as a within-subject DSS trajectory classification task. Each
of the 14 Stress subjects with repeated recordings is assigned a
personal-median DSS threshold; each recording is labelled above vs
below personal median. Classification is leave-one-subject-out with
three candidate classifiers applied to frozen FM embeddings
(Table~\ref{tab:stress_longitudinal}). All nine FM $\times$
classifier cells fall at or below chance; all kappas are negative.

Two interpretations of the Stress failure are jointly supported:
(a) FM representations may lack the capacity to express within-subject
DSS variation at this cohort size, or (b) DSS itself may lack a
per-recording EEG ground truth. Current data cannot distinguish these.
Both interpretations are consistent with SDL: (a) instantiates the
subject-dominance ceiling directly, (b) says the contrast/variance
ratio is $\approx 0$ because the contrast is absent.

\textbf{Within-subject direction consistency.}
Fig.~\ref{fig:within_subj_direction} inspects the geometry of

($0.06$--$0.15$); Stress is indistinguishable from zero
($-0.02$--$0.00$). The Stress result is the absence of any
consistent within-subject trajectory in the frozen representation,
matching the cluster-corrected absence from the pre-registered
PSD anchor test (App.~\ref{app:psd_anchor}).

\textbf{Independent neural-level corroboration: cross-model band
  consensus.} A third line of evidence is available at the
representation level without using classifier performance at all. If
a task has a strong, stable within-subject neural contrast, three
independent architectures should converge on the same neural band;
otherwise they should diverge. We use causal band-stop ablation
(remove one frequency band via a Butterworth filter before feeding
the signal to the frozen FM; measure the cosine distance between
resulting embedding and the unablated baseline) as the per-band
dependence metric. On EEGMAT, LaBraM, REVE, and CBraMod all show
their largest band-stop cosine distances on the alpha band (with REVE
showing the sharpest alpha vs other-band separation). Three
architectures converge on the Klimesch-predicted alpha-ERD band. On
UCSD Stress, LaBraM peaks on beta, REVE on broadband, and CBraMod
shows no band preference --- three architectures diverge on which
band they rely on, and no shared neural target is isolable. We
report single-run per-band distances; cross-seed stability is
tabulated in supplementary.
Fig.~\ref{fig:band_diagnostic} visualises the convergence vs
divergence.

\textbf{Summary.} Same framework, same FMs, same subject-scale,
opposite outcomes at both the behavioural and the representational
level. Within the contrast-strength range spanned by EEGMAT (strong)
and UCSD Stress longitudinal DSS (absent), contrast strength governs
whether FM fine-tuning rescues classification above the
subject-dominance ceiling. Model scale does not rescue labels that
lack a within-subject neural anchor.

\subsection{Architecture-independent ceiling: seven models converge
  within a $5$\,pp band}
\label{sec:results_ceiling}

A natural concern about the picture so far is that the ceiling may be
an artefact of the particular FM architectures tested. We evaluate a
panel of from-scratch EEG architectures on the same 70-recording
subset, the same subject-level protocol, and the same 3-seed
discipline. The panel spans 2017-vintage compact CNNs
\citep{lawhern2018,schirrmeister2017} through modern
attention-convolutional hybrids \citep{song2023eegconformer}, plus
the three fine-tuned FMs and the best class-balanced classical
baseline. Fig.~\ref{fig:ceiling} displays the forest plot.

\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\linewidth]{figures/main/fig_ceiling.pdf}

  class-balanced weighting where applicable. All architectures

\end{figure}

Seven architectures spanning three kilo- to one giga-parameter scales
--- approximately five orders of magnitude --- produce balanced
accuracies clustered within a $\sim\!5$\,pp window on the same
cohort. If the Stress ceiling reflected a representation-quality
limit, scaling should help. It does not: moving from a $3$\,k
parameter 2018 CNN to a $1.4$\,B parameter 2025 FM shifts the mean BA
by at most $6$\,pp, within the single-model seed standard deviation
on three of the six rows. If the ceiling reflected a
pretraining-corpus-quality limit, the pretrained/from-scratch
contrast should matter. It does not: ShallowConvNet (which never saw
any pretrained EEG data) at $0.557$ is identical within noise to
CBraMod FT ($0.548$) and higher than LaBraM FT ($0.524$). If the
ceiling reflected a recent-architecture-innovation limit, the 2017
$\to$ 2025 progression should matter. It does not: the 2017
Schirrmeister ShallowConvNet matches the 2024/2025 FMs.

The ceiling is compatible with a \emph{task-property} explanation:
under subject-level evaluation on this cohort, the available
within-subject neural contrast for per-recording DASS-thresholded
labels is bounded at roughly BA $0.55$, and no architecture available
to us resolves the bounded contrast into higher classification.
Sec.~\ref{sec:results_paired} has already identified this contrast
as the operative variable by the controlled paired comparison;
Sec.~\ref{sec:results_ceiling} is the complementary ruling-out ---
once the contrast is bounded, architectural scale and pretraining
source do not unbound it.

This is a specifically \emph{ceiling-regime} statement. It does not
claim that architectural choice never matters --- on EEGMAT, where
the within-subject contrast is neurally anchored, LaBraM FT reaches
$0.73$ and architecture contributes. In the contrast-bounded regime
of UCSD Stress the architectural-choice lever is functionally
disabled.

\textbf{Scope of the architecture-independence claim.} The forest
plot above is demonstrated on a single bounded cohort (UCSD Stress).
Cross-cohort replication on other contrast-bounded benchmarks is
future work. We note that the aggregate findings reported by
EEG-Bench \citep{eegbench2025} and AdaBrain-Bench
\citep{adabrain2025} across 14+ clinical datasets --- namely that FM
fine-tuning frequently fails to exceed compact from-scratch baselines
on cross-subject splits --- are consistent with this picture at the
aggregate level, though those audits do not decompose their
architecture panels by parameter scale in the way our forest plot
does.

\subsection{What FM pretraining contributes inside the ceiling}
\label{sec:results_fm_adds}

Granted that FMs do not escape the Stress ceiling, what --- if
anything --- do they contribute inside it?

the four families is $\sim\!5$\,pp; no representation advantage
approaches the $15$--$40$\,pp gap reported under trial-level CV.

The $5$\,pp frozen-FM-over-classical advantage is small enough to be
worth interpreting cautiously. In the 70-recording cohort with 14
positives, a shift of that magnitude corresponds to correctly
classifying one to two additional recordings per fold on average, and
the per-fold variance admits multi-pp swings driven by which single
subject enters the validation partition. We report the $5$\,pp as an
observation, consistent with the hypothesis that pretrained FM
embeddings carry a modestly richer representation of the 30-channel
spectral structure than a hand-crafted band-power feature vector.
Crucially, \emph{the $5$\,pp bump lives entirely inside the
$0.44$--$0.58$ architecture-independent band.} FM pretraining does
not move the classification out of the ceiling regime; it places the
classifier slightly higher within it.

On both anchored datasets, FT delivers a rescue step above frozen
LP. On ADFTD, variance decomposition shows the largest label-fraction
increase under FT in our $3 \times 4$ grid ($2.60\% \to 13.25\%$,
single-run) --- the only cell in which fine-tuning substantively
reorganises the representation around the label axis.

\textbf{Two-regime summary.} In the \emph{anchored-contrast} regime,
frozen FM representations outperform classical baselines by
$5$--$8$\,pp and fine-tuning adds a further $5$--$10$\,pp rescue; only
the fine-tuning component is contrast-dependent. In the
\emph{contrast-bounded} regime, frozen FM representations still
contribute a $5$\,pp bump but fine-tuning does not add further rescue
and in some (model, dataset) cells actively erodes the frozen
representation (LaBraM FT on Stress: frozen LP $0.605 \to$ FT $0.524$,
$\sim\!8$\,pp erosion).

This two-regime picture aligns with what concurrent large-scale EEG
FM benchmark audits report at the aggregate level
\citep{eegbench2025,kauffmann2025critique,eegfmbench2025,adabrain2025}.
The paired comparison in Sec.~\ref{sec:results_paired} supplies the
mechanistic layer: the determining factor is the presence or absence
of a within-subject neural anchor.
Sec.~\ref{sec:results_ceiling} supplies the complementary
architectural robustness check: in the contrast-bounded regime, the
anchor's absence cannot be compensated by architectural choice.
