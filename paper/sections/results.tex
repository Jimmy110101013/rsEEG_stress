\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Subject-dominated frozen representations}
\label{sec:results_atlas}

The subject-dominance hypothesis makes a structural prediction
testable independently of any classification outcome: if frozen EEG
FM representations are subject-dominated, variance decomposition of
those representations should find that the subject factor accounts
for the majority of explainable variance and the task-label factor
for a small, near-chance slice. We test this across three FMs
(LaBraM, CBraMod, REVE) and four clinical EEG datasets
(UCSD Stress, EEGMAT, ADFTD, TDBRAIN) --- twelve model$\times$dataset
cells. For each cell we extract a single frozen embedding per
recording by global-mean-pooling the final-layer patch tokens of the
pretrained FM with no gradient flow, no classifier head, and no
fine-tuning.

Fig.~\ref{fig:variance_atlas} triangulates the subject-dominance
claim from three independent representation-level measurements.
Panel A reports the pooled variance decomposition (nested for
between-subject labels, crossed for the within-subject EEGMAT
design): the label fraction is below $6$\% in every cell and the
subject fraction sits between $40$\% and $95$\%. Panel B is a
rank-based corroboration --- the Spearman correlation between the
embedding representational dissimilarity matrix (RDM) and the
subject-identity RDM exceeds the correlation with the
label-identity RDM in 11 of 12 cells; the single exception
(LaBraM $\times$ TDBRAIN) has subject-$r = 0.040$ and label-$r =
0.084$, both near zero. Panel C reports cluster-bootstrap 95\%
confidence intervals on the subject/label variance ratio,
resampling subjects (not recordings) with replacement. All 12 cells
have lower CI bound $> 1$, establishing subject-dominance as
significant at the bootstrap level uniformly across FMs and
datasets. Full per-cell numbers are in Table~\ref{tab:subject_atlas}.

Downstream classification on these representations is constrained
to work against a representation in which task-label variance is a
small minority of the signal. The diagnostic in
Sec.~\ref{sec:results_paired} operationalises \emph{when} that
constraint is escapable.

\subsection{The anchor-contrast diagnostic}
\label{sec:results_paired}

Wang et al.\ \citep{wang2025stress} report $0.9047$ BA on this cohort
under five-fold cross-validation; their fold assignment groups by
recording rather than by subject, and $15$ of $17$ subjects contribute
multiple recordings. Under subject-level StratifiedGroupKFold on the
same cohort the three FMs converge to a $0.52$--$0.58$
balanced-accuracy band (Fig.~\ref{fig:honest_funnel}, three seeds per
model). For the canonical LaBraM fine-tuning recipe, real FT
($0.443 \pm 0.083$) does not exceed label-permutation null
($0.497 \pm 0.086$; one-sided $p = 0.70$, ten permutations). This is
the ceiling the diagnostic below must explain.

\begin{figure}[!t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\linewidth]{figures/main/fig_honest_funnel.pdf}
\caption{UCSD Stress ceiling. Left: trial-level CV baseline
  ($0.9047$, \citep{wang2025stress}). Middle: subject-level CV on the
  same cohort converges to a $0.52$--$0.58$ band across three FMs and
  three seeds. Right: LaBraM fine-tuning does not exceed
  label-permutation null (three seeds $\times$ ten permutations,
  one-sided $p = 0.70$). Horizontal dashed line: chance.}
\label{fig:honest_funnel}
\end{figure}

The diagnostic is a paired comparison on two arms with the same three
FMs and the same subject-respecting evaluation. The \emph{only}
structural difference between arms is the neural contrast underlying
the label. Following the anchoring protocol
(Sec.~\ref{sec:methods_anchor}), contrast strength is established
externally, before reading any FM result. Both arms are reported as
three-seed mean $\pm$ std.

\textbf{EEGMAT anchor.} Serial-subtraction mental arithmetic produces
canonical posterior alpha desynchronisation
\citep{klimesch1999,pfurtscheller1979,klimesch2012update}. Robust,
spectrally localised, reliably induced across participants.

\textbf{UCSD Stress longitudinal DSS anchor.} A systematic search
returns no published per-recording within-subject EEG correlate of
DASS-21 or Daily Stress Scale scores. The Komarov--Ko--Jung paper
itself is a group-level longitudinal correlational study
(Sec.~\ref{sec:methods_dass_scope}), not a per-recording
within-subject classification benchmark. As a pre-registered internal
check, a cluster-based permutation $F$-test on log-PSDs
(Sec.~\ref{sec:methods_anchor}, 1000 permutations) returned no
cluster-corrected significant cluster on the 70-recording cohort
(minimum cluster $p = 0.096$; subthreshold theta--alpha effect at
$g = +0.82$, not surviving correction). At the pre-registered
threshold the external anchor is empirically absent on this cohort.
Full details in App.~\ref{app:psd_anchor}.

Under SDL the two anchors jointly predict the experimental outcome:
EEGMAT should rescue, Stress longitudinal DSS should fail.

\textbf{Arm 1 --- EEGMAT rescue succeeds.} Paired rest vs
serial-subtraction mental-arithmetic recordings for each of $36$
EEGMAT subjects ($72$ recordings). All three FMs fine-tuned on this
within-subject contrast rescue above classical and frozen-LP
baselines: LaBraM $0.731 \pm 0.021$, REVE $0.727 \pm 0.035$, CBraMod
$0.620 \pm 0.058$. The three-FM agreement is itself diagnostic ---
rescue is not a LaBraM-specific artefact but a consistent response
of pretrained EEG representations to an anchored within-subject
contrast. The rescue does not reorganise the frozen representation
around the label axis: the pooled label fraction shifts only from
$5.35\% \to 3.34\%$ under FT on EEGMAT, and the subject fraction
rises from $71.2\% \to 82.8\%$. The rescue operates at the classifier
head while the embedding itself remains subject-dominated, with the
label axis projected onto the existing subject-structured
representation --- ``projection, not rewrite.''

\textbf{Arm 2 --- Stress longitudinal DSS fails.} We reframe UCSD
Stress as a within-subject DSS trajectory classification task. Each
of the 14 Stress subjects with repeated recordings is assigned a
personal-median DSS threshold; each recording is labelled above vs
below personal median. Classification is leave-one-subject-out with
three candidate classifiers applied to frozen FM embeddings
(Table~\ref{tab:stress_longitudinal}). All nine FM $\times$
classifier cells fall at or below chance.

Two interpretations of the Stress failure are jointly supported by
these data: (a) FM representations may lack the capacity to express
within-subject DSS variation at this cohort size, or (b) DSS itself
may lack a per-recording EEG ground truth. Both are consistent with
SDL: (a) instantiates the subject-dominance ceiling directly, (b)
says the contrast/variance ratio is $\approx 0$ because the contrast
is absent.

\textbf{Within-subject trajectory geometry.}
Fig.~\ref{fig:within_subj_direction} computes the mean pairwise
cosine similarity of per-subject condition-shift vectors in the
frozen representation. EEGMAT is positive across all three FMs
($0.06$--$0.15$); Stress is indistinguishable from zero
($-0.02$--$0.00$). The Stress result is the absence of any
consistent within-subject trajectory in the frozen representation,
matching the cluster-corrected absence from the PSD anchor test
(App.~\ref{app:psd_anchor}).

\textbf{Independent neural-band corroboration.} A third line of
evidence is available at the representation level without using
classifier performance. Causal band-stop ablation removes one
frequency band via a Butterworth filter before the frozen FM and
measures the cosine distance between the ablated and unablated
embedding. On EEGMAT, LaBraM, REVE, and CBraMod all show their
largest band-stop distances on alpha --- three architectures
converge on the Klimesch-predicted band
(Fig.~\ref{fig:band_diagnostic}, top). On UCSD Stress, LaBraM peaks
on beta, REVE on broadband, CBraMod shows no band preference ---
three architectures diverge on which band they rely on, and no
shared neural target is isolable (Fig.~\ref{fig:band_diagnostic},
bottom). This diagnostic uses no classifier output and independently
corroborates the paired behavioural outcome.

\textbf{Architectural robustness of the ceiling.}
The ceiling could in principle be an artefact of the three FM
architectures we tested. We evaluate a panel of seven architectures
spanning five orders of magnitude of parameter count on the same
70-recording cohort under the same subject-level protocol
(Table~\ref{tab:architectures}, Fig.~\ref{fig:ceiling}). All converge
to a $0.44$--$0.58$ band. A 2017 Schirrmeister ShallowConvNet
($\sim\!40$\,k parameters, never pretrained) matches the 2024/2025
pretrained FMs within seed noise; moving from $3$\,k to $1.4$\,B
parameters shifts the mean BA by at most $6$\,pp. Scale, pretraining
source, and architecture vintage fail to unbound the ceiling ---
the ceiling is a task property. In the anchored regime
(EEGMAT, $0.73$) architecture contributes; in the contrast-bounded
regime it is functionally disabled. Cross-cohort replication on
other contrast-bounded benchmarks is future work; the
aggregate pattern reported across 14+ clinical datasets by
EEG-Bench \citep{eegbench2025} and AdaBrain-Bench
\citep{adabrain2025} --- FM fine-tuning frequently failing to
exceed compact from-scratch baselines under cross-subject splits ---
is consistent with the present forest plot at the aggregate level.

Same framework, same FMs, same subject-scale, opposite outcomes at
the behavioural and the representational level. Within the
contrast-strength range spanned by EEGMAT (strong) and UCSD Stress
longitudinal DSS (absent), contrast strength governs whether FM
fine-tuning rescues classification above the subject-dominance
ceiling. Scale does not rescue labels without a within-subject
neural anchor.

\subsection{Contributions of pretraining within the ceiling}
\label{sec:results_fm_adds}

FMs do not escape the ceiling; what --- if anything --- do they
contribute inside it? Table~\ref{tab:fm_value_cross_dataset} holds
the classifier head constant and compares three representation
families across one contrast-bounded and two contrast-anchored
datasets. For UCSD Stress, classical and frozen-LP numbers are
measured on the 70-recording cohort with class-balanced sample
weighting (3-seed for classical GBT, 8-seed for frozen LP). For
EEGMAT and ADFTD, classical and frozen-LP reference values are
approximate ranges from published literature; FT numbers are 3-seed
means (Stress, EEGMAT) or single-run (ADFTD).

\centering
\small

  Stress numbers are class-balanced measured on the 70-recording
  cohort; EEGMAT and ADFTD classical/frozen LP entries are

UCSD Stress (contrast-bounded)      & $0.553 \pm 0.081$ & $\mathbf{0.605 \pm 0.032}$ & $0.577 \pm 0.051$ \\

ADFTD (anchored, cross-subj.)       & $\sim\!0.60$      & $\sim\!0.65$              & $\mathbf{0.70}$ \\
\bottomrule

Two regimes emerge. In the \emph{anchored-contrast} regime (EEGMAT,
ADFTD), frozen FM linear probes outperform classical baselines by
$5$--$8$\,pp, and fine-tuning adds a further $5$--$10$\,pp rescue;
only the fine-tuning component is contrast-dependent. In the
\emph{contrast-bounded} regime (UCSD Stress), frozen FM
representations still contribute a $5$\,pp bump over classical
baselines, but fine-tuning does not add further rescue and in some
cells actively erodes the frozen representation (LaBraM on Stress:
frozen LP $0.605 \to$ FT $0.524$, $\sim\!8$\,pp erosion). On ADFTD,
variance decomposition shows the largest label-fraction increase
under FT in our $3 \times 4$ grid ($2.60\% \to 13.25\%$, single-run)
--- the only cell where fine-tuning substantively reorganises the
representation around the label axis, consistent with the
cross-subject alpha-slowing signature of the AD label.

The $5$\,pp frozen-LP advantage on Stress is small enough to warrant
interpretive caution: at $70$ recordings with $14$ positives, it
corresponds to correctly classifying one to two additional
recordings per fold. We report it as consistent with pretrained FM
embeddings carrying a modestly richer representation of 30-channel
spectral structure than a hand-crafted band-power feature vector ---
but the bump lives entirely inside the $0.44$--$0.58$
architecture-independent band. FM pretraining does not move the
classification out of the ceiling regime; it places the classifier
slightly higher within it.

This two-regime picture is the mechanistic layer underneath what
concurrent large-scale EEG FM benchmark audits report at the
aggregate level
\citep{eegbench2025,kauffmann2025critique,eegfmbench2025,adabrain2025}.
The paired diagnostic in Sec.~\ref{sec:results_paired} identifies
the determining factor as the presence or absence of a within-subject
neural anchor; the architectural robustness check there establishes
that in the contrast-bounded regime the anchor's absence cannot be
compensated by architectural choice.
