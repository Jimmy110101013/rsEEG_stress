\paragraph{EEG foundation models.}
LaBraM~\citep{labram2024}, CBraMod~\citep{cbramod2025}, REVE~\citep{reve2024},
BIOT, and BENDR pretrain transformer encoders on large unlabelled EEG corpora
via masked patch reconstruction or contrastive objectives. Reported
downstream tasks span sleep staging, motor imagery, and clinical
classification.

\paragraph{Cross-subject benchmarks reveal a consistent FM ceiling.}
Three independent 2025--2026 benchmarks have reported that EEG foundation
models fail to beat classical baselines on cross-subject affective and
cognitive tasks: Brain4FMs~\citep{brain4fms2026},
EEG-FM-Bench~\citep{eegfmbench2025}, and AdaBrain-Bench~\citep{adabrain2025}.
Our work complements these benchmarks by providing the \emph{mechanistic}
explanation: subject-identity dominance in the learned feature space.

\paragraph{Subject leakage in EEG cross-validation.}
The distinction between trial-level (random) and subject-level (grouped)
cross-validation is well-known in the EEG ML literature
\citep[e.g.,][]{varoquaux2017}, but trial-level splits remain common in
published EEG stress classification work. We document the resulting
inflation systematically across three FMs.

\paragraph{Variance decomposition for representation analysis.}
$\eta^{2}$ from one-way ANOVA is a standard effect-size measure for
identifying the dominant source of variance in a feature set
\citep{cohen1973}.
% TODO: cite recent neuroimaging work using $\eta^2$ for FM analysis.
The ``brain fingerprinting'' literature \citep{finn2015} establishes that
within-subject features are remarkably stable, providing precedent for
subject-identity-dominance findings. We extend this from rsfMRI to
foundation-model EEG embeddings.

\paragraph{Subject-adversarial training and domain generalisation.}
Gradient reversal layer (GRL)~\citep{ganin2015} and related domain
adversarial methods aim to remove nuisance variables from learned
representations. The LEAD framework~\citep{lead2024} applies this to
EEG-as-domain. We evaluate both on our task and find they degrade rather
than improve performance, consistent with the hypothesis that the diagnostic
signal is too weak to survive subject-information removal.

Public resting-state EEG stress datasets are scarce. EEGMAT, SAM40, and
EDPMSC use acute stress induction (mental arithmetic) rather than chronic
stress, and none use validated psychometric labels such as DASS. Our UCSD
Stress dataset fills this gap with longitudinal recordings under both DASS
and DSS instruments, but at the cost of a small subject count
($N{=}17$) --- a constraint we treat as part of the diagnosis.
